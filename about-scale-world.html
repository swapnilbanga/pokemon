About Bandai Pokémon Scale World

Pokémon Scale World is Bandai’s line of 1/20 scale figures that makes your display feel like a slice of the Pokémon world. If you care about how Pokemon look next to each other, this is the line for you.

Bandai releases these as PVC figure sets built around regions and themes — Kanto, Johto, Galar, Hisui and more. Some sets focus on trainers and their teams, others on popular or larger Pokémon, and there are occasional Premium Bandai releases for special pieces. Over time you can build the regions, scenes, or favourite teams that matter to you.

Is it still in production?

The older regions are no longer being released or manufactured. The newer regions are being released as they are announced. Thus, the older regions like Kanto and Johto are hard to come by at good prices.

How it compares to Moncolle and other figure lines

If you have collected Moncolle (Takara Tomy) or other Pokémon figure lines before, you know the trade-off: there is a huge variety, but the sizes do not always match how big those Pokémon really are next to each other. They look better individually and in my opinion suited for completionists.

Pokémon Scale World does the opposite. It sticks to a consistent 1/20 scale across the whole line, so the size relationships actually make sense. That one decision changes how the collection feels:

  • Large Pokémon like Groudon or Lugia feel genuinely imposing.
  • Human trainers fit properly next to their partners, which helps when creating dioramas or gym battles.
